# International Organisations

## 1. ITU (International Telecommunication Union)
The UN agency for information and communication technologies, based in Geneva.
*   **Role:** Allocates global radio spectrum and satellite orbits. Maintains the **Radio [Regulations](03_Rules_&_Regulations.md) (RR)**.
*   **Definition of Amateur Service (Art. 1.56):**
    *   "A radiocommunication service for the purpose of **self-training**, **intercommunication** and **technical investigations** carried out by amateurs, that is, by duly authorized persons interested in radio technique solely with a **personal aim** and **without pecuniary interest**."
*   **Regions:** The world is divided into 3 regions.
    *   **Region 1:** Europe, Africa, Middle East, Northern Asia (including Russia). **[Netherlands](07_Operating_Rules_Netherlands.md) is in Region 1.**
    *   **Region 2:** The Americas (North and South).
    *   **Region 3:** Asia-Pacific (Australia, China, India, Oceania).

## 2. CEPT (Conférence Européenne des Administrations des Postes et des Télécommunications)
European organization of postal and telecommunications administrations.
*   **Role:** Harmonizes regulations within Europe.
*   **Recommendations:**
    *   **T/R 61-01:** Allows temporary operation in other CEPT countries without a separate license (Guest operation).
        *   *ID Rule:* Use host prefix followed by "/", e.g., **DL/PA0ABC**.
    *   **T/R 61-02:** Defines the **HAREC** (Harmonised Amateur Radio Examination Certificate) syllabus.
    *   **ECC-REC(05)06:** Harmonization for Novice licenses.

## 3. IARU (International Amateur Radio Union)
The global federation of national amateur radio societies (like VERON/VRZA in NL).
*   **Role:** Represents the interests of amateur radio to the [ITU](05_Emission_Classes_ITU.md).
*   **Band Plans:** Coordinated by IARU Region 1 for Europe. These are "gentlemen's agreements" (recommendations), not law, but highly respected.

## 4. HAREC
**Harmonised Amateur Radio Examination Certificate.**
*   The standard for the "Full" license exam.
*   Passing the F-exam in the [Netherlands](07_Operating_Rules_Netherlands.md) grants a HAREC certificate, recognized in many other countries for issuing a license.
