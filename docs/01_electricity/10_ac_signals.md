# AC Signals and Noise

## 1. Sinusoidal Signals (Sinusvormige signalen)
The fundamental waveform of Alternating Current (AC) and Radio Frequency (RF). AC signals periodically reverse direction.

### Key Parameters
*   **Period ($T$):** The time it takes to complete one full cycle. Unit: Seconds ($s$).
*   **Frequency ($f$):** The number of cycles per second. Unit: Hertz ($Hz$).
    *   **Formula:** $f = \frac{1}{T}$ and $T = \frac{1}{f}$.
*   **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** Rotation speed in radians per second.
    *   **Formula:** $\omega = 2\pi f$.
*   **Amplitude ($A$ or $U_{max}$):** The maximum deviation from zero (peak value).
*   **Peak-to-Peak ($U_{pp}$):** Difference between maximum positive and maximum negative value ($2 \times Amplitude$).

### Voltage Values
*   **Instantaneous Value ($u(t)$):** The value at a specific moment in time.
    *   $u(t) = U_{max} \sin(\omega t + \varphi)$.
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** The distance a wave travels in one period.
    *   $\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$ (where $v$ is velocity).
    *   For radio waves, $v = c$ (speed of light, approx $300,000 \text{ km/s}$). See [EM Fields](09_fields.md).
*   **Average Value ($U_{gem}$):**
    *   Over a full period, the average of a pure sine wave is **0**.
    *   Over a half period (rectified), $U_{gem} \approx 0.637 \times U_{max}$ ($\frac{2}{\pi}$).
*   **Effective Value (RMS, $U_{eff}$):** The DC voltage that produces the same heat power in a resistor.
    *   **Formula:** $U_{eff} = \frac{U_{max}}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707 \times U_{max}$.
    *   Conversely: $U_{max} = U_{eff} \times \sqrt{2} \approx 1.41 \times U_{eff}$.
    *   *Note:* Mains voltage (230V) is an RMS value. The peak is $\approx 325V$.

### Phase ($\varphi$)
*   **Absolute Phase:** The progress of a wave within its cycle (0 to 360 degrees or $2\pi$ radians).
*   **Phase Difference (Faseverschil):** The shift between two waves of the same frequency.
    *   **In Phase:** Peaks align ($\Delta \varphi = 0^\circ$).
    *   **Anti-phase:** Peak aligns with Trough ($\Delta \varphi = 180^\circ$).
    *   **Quadrature:** Shift of $90^\circ$ ($\lambda/4$).

## 2. Mixed Signals (AC + DC)
An AC signal can be superimposed on a DC voltage (offset).
*   **Result:** The waveform shifts up or down on the Y-axis.
*   **Separation:** A capacitor blocks the DC component, allowing only the AC component to pass.

## 3. Non-Sinusoidal Signals
Complex periodic waves can be constructed from a sum of sine waves (Fundamental + Harmonics). This concept is **Fourier Analysis**.

*   **Square Wave (Blokgolf):** Fundamental + **Odd** harmonics ($3f, 5f, 7f...$).
    *   Amplitude of harmonic $n$ drops as $1/n$.
*   **Triangle Wave (Driehoeksgolf):** Fundamental + **Odd** harmonics.
    *   Amplitude of harmonic $n$ drops as $1/n^2$ (faster decay than square).
*   **Sawtooth (Zaagtand):** Fundamental + **Even AND Odd** harmonics.

## 4. Noise (Ruis)
*   **Thermal Noise:** Generated by thermal agitation of electrons in conductors.
    $$P_n = k \cdot T \cdot B$$
    *   $k$ = Boltzmann's constant.
    *   $T$ = Temperature (Kelvin).
    *   $B$ = Bandwidth (Hz).
*   **Shot Noise:** Random fluctuations in current across junctions (diodes/transistors).
*   **External Noise:**
    *   **QRN:** Natural/Atmospheric (Static, Lightning).
    *   **QRM:** Man-made (Machinery, Electronics, Interference).
