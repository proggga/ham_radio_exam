# AC Signals and Noise

## 1. Sinusoidal Signals (Sinusvormige signalen)
The fundamental waveform of Alternating Current (AC) and Radio Frequency (RF).

### Key Parameters
*   **Amplitude ($U_{max}$):** The peak voltage.
*   **Period ($T$):** Time for one complete cycle (seconds).
*   **Frequency ($f$):** Cycles per second (Hertz).
    $$f = \frac{1}{T}$$
*   **Angular Frequency ($\omega$):** Rotation speed in radians per second.
    $$\omega = 2\pi f$$
*   **Effective Value (RMS):** The DC voltage that produces the same heat.
    $$U_{eff} = \frac{U_{max}}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707 \times U_{max}$$
*   **Phase:** Relative timing between waves (degrees or radians).

## 2. Non-Sinusoidal Signals
Complex waves can be constructed from sine waves (**Fourier Analysis**).
*   **Square Wave (Blokgolf):** Fundamental + Odd harmonics (3f, 5f...) with amplitude $1/n$.
*   **Triangle Wave (Driehoeksgolf):** Fundamental + Odd harmonics with amplitude $1/n^2$.
*   **Sawtooth (Zaagtand):** Fundamental + Even AND Odd harmonics.

## 3. Noise (Ruis)
*   **Thermal Noise:** Generated by thermal agitation of electrons in conductors.
    $$P_n = k \cdot T \cdot B$$
    *   Depends on Temperature ($T$) and Bandwidth ($B$).
*   **Shot Noise:** Random fluctuations in current across junctions (diodes/transistors).
*   **External Noise:**
    *   **QRN:** Natural/Atmospheric (Static, Lightning).
    *   **QRM:** Man-made (Machinery, Electronics, Interference).
