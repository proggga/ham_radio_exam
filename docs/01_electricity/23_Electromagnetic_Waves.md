# Electromagnetic Waves

Radio waves are electromagnetic waves consisting of oscillating **Electric ($E$)** and **Magnetic ($H$)** fields.

## Structure
*   **Orientation**: The $E$ and $H$ fields are perpendicular ($90^\circ$) to each other and perpendicular to the direction of propagation.
*   **Transverse Wave**: The oscillation is crosswise to the direction of travel.

## Properties

### Velocity ($c$)
In a vacuum, they travel at the speed of light.
*   $c \approx 300,000,000 \text{ m/s}$ ($3 \times 10^8$ m/s).

### Wavelength ($\lambda$)
The distance the wave travels in one complete cycle.
*   **Formula**: $$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$
*   **Practical**: $\lambda_{(m)} \approx \frac{300}{f_{(MHz)}}$

### Polarization
Defined by the orientation of the **[Electric Field](25_Electric_Field.md) ($E$)**.
*   **Horizontal**: E-field oscillates horizontally (e.g., [Dipole](../06_antennas/02_The_Dipole_Antenna.md) parallel to ground).
*   **Vertical**: E-field oscillates vertically (e.g., Vertical Ground Plane).
*   **Circular**: E-field vector rotates.
