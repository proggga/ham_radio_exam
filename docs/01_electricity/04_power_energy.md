# Power and Energy

## 1. Electric Power (Vermogen)
Power is the rate at which energy is used or dissipated.
*   **Symbol:** $P$
*   **Unit:** Watt ($W$) = Joule per second ($J/s$).

### Formulas
Derived from [Ohm's Law](02_ohm_law.md):
1.  **Basic:** $P = U \times I$
2.  **Substitution ($U=I \times R$):** $P = I^2 \times R$ (Heat loss in a resistor)
3.  **Substitution ($I=U/R$):** $P = U \cdot (U / R) \rightarrow \mathbf{P = \frac{U^2}{R}}$

## 2. Dissipation
When current flows through a resistor, electrical power is converted into heat. This is called **dissipation**.
*   A resistor must be rated for the power it dissipates (e.g., 1/4 Watt, 5 Watt).
*   If $P_{dissipated} > P_{rated}$, the component will overheat and fail (magic smoke).

## 3. Energy (Arbeid/Energie)
Energy is total power used over a period of time.
*   **Symbol:** $W$ (Work) or $E$.
*   **Unit:** Joule ($J$) or Watt-second ($Ws$).
    *   Practical unit: **Kilowatt-hour (kWh)**.
    *   $1 \text{ kWh} = 1000 \text{ W} \times 3600 \text{ s} = 3,600,000 \text{ J}$.

### Formula
$$W = P \times t$$
(Energy = Power $\times$ Time)
