# Optoelectronic Devices

Optoelectronic devices interact with light, either emitting it or detecting it.

## Light Emitting Devices

### LED (Light Emitting Diode)
*   **Function**: Converts electrical energy into light via electroluminescence.
*   **Structure**: A PN junction diode made from specific semiconductor materials (Gallium Arsenide, Gallium Phosphide).
*   **Operation**: Emits light when **Forward Biased**. The color depends on the band gap of the material.
*   **Application**: Indicators, Displays (Seven-segment), Lighting.

### Laser Diode
*   **Function**: Emits coherent light (LASER).
*   **Use**: Fiber optic communications, Disc readers.

## Light Detecting Devices

### Photodiode
*   **Function**: Converts light into electrical current.
*   **Operation**: Operated in **Reverse Bias**. Light striking the junction creates electron-hole pairs, causing a small leakage current proportional to light intensity.
*   **Use**: Light sensors, remote control receivers.

### Photovoltaic Cell (Solar Cell)
*   **Function**: Generates voltage from light energy.
*   **Operation**: A large area PN junction. Light energy pushes electrons across the barrier, creating a potential difference.
*   **Use**: Power generation ([[Solar Indices & Space Weather|Solar Power]] for portable stations).

### Optocoupler (Optoisolator)
*   **Structure**: An [[LED (Light Emitting Diode)|LED]] and a Phototransistor inside a light-tight package.
*   **Function**: Transfers signals between circuits using light while maintaining electrical **Isolation**.
*   **Use**: Interfacing radios to computers (PTT/[[CW Abbreviations & Prosigns|CW]] keying) to prevent ground loops.
