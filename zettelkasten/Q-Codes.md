# Q-Codes

Q-codes are three-letter codes starting with Q. They were originally developed for telegraphy to save time but are widely used in voice communications as well.

A question mark after the code turns it into a question (in CW).

## Common Q-Codes

*   **QRA**: What is the name of your station? / The name of my station is...
*   **QRG**: Will you tell me my exact frequency? / Your exact frequency is...
*   **QRK**: What is the readability of my signals? / The readability of your signals is (1-5).
*   **QRL**: Are you busy? / I am busy.
*   **QRM**: Is my transmission being interfered with? / You are being interfered with (Man-made interference). See [[Types of Interference]].
*   **QRN**: Are you troubled by static? / I am troubled by static (Natural noise). See [[AC Signals & Noise]].
*   **QRO**: Shall I increase transmitter power? / Increase transmitter power.
*   **QRP**: Shall I decrease transmitter power? / Decrease transmitter power (Low power operation).
*   **QRT**: Shall I stop sending? / Stop sending (Closing down).
*   **QRV**: Are you ready? / I am ready.
*   **QRX**: When will you call me again? / Call me again at ... (Wait).
*   **QRZ**: Who is calling me? / You are being called by...
*   **QSB**: Are my signals fading? / Your signals are fading. See [[The Ionosphere]].
*   **QSL**: Can you acknowledge receipt? / I acknowledge receipt (Confirm contact).
*   **QSO**: Can you communicate with ... direct? / I can communicate with ... (Radio contact).
*   **QSY**: Shall I change to transmission on another frequency? / Change to transmission on another frequency.
*   **QTH**: What is your position? / My position is... (Location).

## Usage Note
In voice operation, these are often used as nouns or verbs: "I have a lot of QRM here" or "Let's QSY to 14.250".
